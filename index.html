Canada

  • Located in North America, it is the second-largest country in the world by land area.
  • Famous for natural landscapes like the Rocky Mountains, Niagara Falls, and vast forests.
  • Multicultural cities such as Toronto, Vancouver, and Montreal offer rich cultural experiences.
  • Known for winter sports like ice hockey and its progressive policies.
  • Shares the longest undefended border in the world with the United States.

France

  • Located in Western Europe, it is renowned for art, fashion, and cuisine.
  • Paris, the capital, houses landmarks like the Eiffel Tower and the Louvre Museum.
  • Known for romantic ambiance, historic castles, and wine regions like Bordeaux and Champagne.
  • Highly influential in global art, history, and gastronomy.
  • One of the most visited countries in the world.

Germany

  • Situated in the heart of Europe, celebrated for engineering and innovation.
  • Features diverse landscapes like the Black Forest and Bavarian Alps.
  • Cities like Berlin, Munich, and Frankfurt blend history with modernity.
  • Known for cultural contributions in music, philosophy, and Oktoberfest.
  • Plays a key role in global politics and the European Union.

USA

  • Located in North America, it is a vast and diverse country.
  • Known for landmarks like the Grand Canyon, Statue of Liberty, and Hollywood.
  • Features natural beauty from Alaska’s wilderness to Florida’s beaches.
  • Bustling cities like New York, Los Angeles, and Chicago add to its charm.
  • A global leader in entertainment, innovation, and higher education.
